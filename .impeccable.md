## Design Context

### Users
Operators, founders, and workflow designers use VoiceToFlow to turn messy operational conversations into structured flows, notes, docs, and tasks. They work inside the editor while planning real follow-ups, so dense text must stay readable and actions must stay close at hand.

### Brand Personality
Direct, calm, operational. The interface should feel trustworthy, precise, and useful rather than flashy. The emotional goal is steady control: users should feel like they can capture messy work and shape it into a clear system.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode first, with a quiet workspace and high-contrast writing surfaces. Use restrained color to separate note, doc, and task content, but keep authored text visually primary. Avoid generic admin-dashboard chrome, loud gradients, and anything that makes the workspace feel like a demo instead of a tool.

### Design Principles
- Make authored text the first thing that reads; controls should support the content, not compete with it.
- Use spacing, contrast, and section tone to separate notes, docs, and tasks at a glance.
- Keep interaction copy plain and operational.
- Let status and metadata scan quickly through compact chips and clearly grouped actions.
- Prefer calm, durable UI decisions over decorative novelty.
